Organic farming is a method of agriculture that relies on natural processes and eco-friendly techniques to grow crops and raise animals. It avoids the use of synthetic chemicals like fertilizers, pesticides, and genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Instead, organic farmers use compost, crop rotation, biological pest control, and other sustainable practices to maintain soil health and promote biodiversity. This approach aims to produce healthy food while protecting the environment and ensuring long-term agricultural productivity.
